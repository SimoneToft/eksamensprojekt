SAZERAC

FLAVOR PROFILE

This cocktail packs a real punch. A healthy amount of cognac is mixed with sugar
and absinthe and is dashed with bitters before being served with a twisted lemon peel.
Sazerac was invented in 1838, and is the oldest known cocktail!

GOES WELL WITH

GNOCCHI

INGREDIENTS

- ice
- 1 tsp absinthe
- 25 ml sugar syrup
- Â½ tsp Peychaud's bitters
- a shake of Angostura bitters
- 100 ml rye whiskey
- strip of orange zest

HOW TO MIX IT

STEP 1

Take your glass and pour in the absinthe.
Swirl around to coat the inside of the glass.

STEP 2

Pour the sugar syrup, both types of bitters and whiskey
into a large mixing glass. Add 15ml cold water and a good
handful of ice. Stir until the outside of the vessel feels
really cold. Taste; if it's too sweet or strong, keep
stirring to dilute it further.

STEP 3

Strain into your prepared glass. Twist a piece of orange
zest over a glass to release a little of the citrus oils,
then drop it in.
