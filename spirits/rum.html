RUM

WHAT DOES IT TASTE LIKE?

Rum is made in a diverse range of cultures, which have their own techniques and methods for producing rum. This results in rums offering a large variety of flavors and experiences. In general, rum's taste is reminiscent of sugar but its sweet flavor is significantly lowered through the distillation process. This results in it being quite dry, but still offers a distinctive nectarous flavor. Regardless, rum will will taste like it's raw ingredients, in a way that reflects how the ingredients were prepared. For example, rum that is made from molasses will usually have a more rounded and bold flavor, where as those made with sugar cane juice may have a more herbaceous flavor. When rum is aged in wooden barrels, the wood will often give the rum some of its textures and flavors. The result of this is often described as a caramel flavor with a smoother mouthfeel. The aging process of rum will often lead to the development of aromas of spice, leather, tobacco, and even candied fruit. Finally, spiced rum, as well as some dark rums, will incorporate various flavorings to produce a desired experience.

WHAT IS IT?

Rum is an alcoholic spirit that's made by distilling fermented molasses or sugarcane juice. Although the rum production industry is largely unregulated, it's typically aged for at least 8 months or two years before it can be sold. However, there are many dark rums that are also aged for far longer.

WHITE RUM

Occasionally referred to as “plata”, “blanco”, or even “blanc”, white rum is usually aged despite its appearance. Sometimes, white rum is aged in steel vats to prevent it from taking on additional flavors. Although, it can also be aged in wooden casks and then filtered to remove the color and any impurities. Light rum is often mild in flavor but quite sweet. White rum is often used as a cocktail ingredient, which is why it's one of the most popular types of rum. It is rare that light rum is above 40% ABV.

GOLD RUM

Gold rum is often viewed as a compromise between clear rum and dark aged rums. Usually, it's aged for around two years using white oak ex-bourbon barrels. Although gold rum will have a richer flavor than light rum, it rarely delivers anything overly complex. Gold rum, is therefore also often used as an alternative for cocktails. Gold rum is often seen as easier to drink neat and can also be more refreshing than dark rum.

DARK RUM

Dark rum is a complex classification that can be occasionally somewhat vague. However, it is also seen as one of the most prestigious types of rum. Certain rums can be darkened by using caramel and its flavor is adjusted with additives like vanilla essence. Although it's aged, it might not be for much longer than gold rum. Dark rum is usually aged for a number of years in oak barrels, sometimes the barrels are charred for a bolder flavor.

CAN BE USED FOR:

DAIQUIRI, DARK AND STORMY, LONG ISLAND ICED TEA, MOJITO, PINA COLADA, MAI TAI
