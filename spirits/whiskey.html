WHISKEY

WHAT IS IT?

Whiskey (or whisky) is a very famous distilled spirit. It is made from fermented grain and is usually matured in a wooden cask. It is usually distilled for 2-3 or more years, but it can heavily vary depending on the desired outcome. Different use of malt or types of grain, makes this distilled spirit very broad in flavor and brand variation. There are several different types of whiskey, the main ones being scotch and bourbon. Whiskey also has a big variation when it comes to the type of grain or malt it uses in the making process. Grain whiskey is made from different types of grain, mainly wheat, rye or corn. These make for different types of whiskey altogether. Corn is for example mainly used in courbon for a sweet flavor, and rye is used for a spicier and drier whiskey. Malt whiskey is only made from malted barley. It has a higher production cost, because it is made in batches rather than a continuous production. Although this does not reflect the quality of the product. Single malt is when the product comes from the same distillery, whereas a blended malt is the combined product of multiple single malts from different distilleries. This is also where the variation of the word "whiskey/whisky" comes from. In Scotland and Canada it is spelled "Whiskey", and in the United States and Ireland it is typically spelled "Whisky".

SCOTCH

This is one of the more popular types of whiskey. It is produced and distilled in Scotland, where the name also derives from. Whiskey is a term derived from the Gaelic "uisge beatha" which translates to "water of life". Scotch whiskey is known for being smokey and richer. This all depends on the individual product of course. A scotch whiskey has to age for a minimum of 3 year, in an oak barrel. It has to be at least 40% alcohol in terms of volume. Popular distilleries in Scotland include Johnie Walker, Glenfiddich and Glenlivet.

BOURBON

This whiskey is known for being sweeter and lighter than the Scottish counterpart. This is also reflected in the different drinks that use Bourbon as its main ingredient. Bourbon is an American type of whiskey, mainly distilled in Tennessee or Kentucky. It is in fact also the only American native spirit, and has been since 1964. Whereas Scotch has a minimum aging requirement, bourbon does not. Notable distillers of bourbon include Makers Mark, Jim Beam, Bulleit and Jack Daniel's.
