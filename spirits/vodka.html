VODKA

WHAT DOES IT TASTE LIKE?

Vodka has a reputation for being tasteless. However, it can be difficult to drink on its own due to its high alcohol content, which can cause a burning sensation in your mouth and throat. The “mouthfeel” of a vodka can depend on a varriety of factors like its distilling process, impurities in the vodka, and the price of the vodka.

WHAT IS IT?

Vodka can be distilled from a variety of fermented grains and vegetables, you can therefoe get vodka varieties like rye vodka, corn vodka, potato vodka, and more. The fermentation process creates a product with only about 16 percent alcohol by volume (ABV) — which is too low for spirits. Getting the number higher requires the liquis to be distilled, or heated in a container called a still. Alcohol boils at a lower temperature than water, so the evaporated alcohol is collected, separating it from the water. Most vodka is around 30 to 40 percent ABV. When you distill vodka, you get rid of impurities, also called congeners. Sometimes you will se vodka being promoted as being distilled multiple times. It would seem like the vodka has less impurities, but distilling vodka multiple times can make the vodka stronger. Which can cause vodka to have a bitter aftertaste, which is why most vodkas only go through 2 or 3 distillations.

VARIATIONS

Due to vodkas lack of flavor, it is the perfect spirit for cocktails. You can also get a large variety of flavored vodkas
