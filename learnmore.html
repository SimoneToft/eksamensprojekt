HOW TO LAYER DRINKS

Floating ingredients is a simple technique to learn, but it can produce beautiful cocktails and drinks, like Tequila Sunrise. However, you can layer many different drinks and cocktails if you simply understand how it works.
To successfully layer drinks, you simply need to know the density of your liquids. For example, in a Tequila Sunrise you use grenadine, which is a thick sugary syrup, which is why it sinks to the bottom.
Sugar and alcohol percentage both play a part in whether a liqueur will float to the top or sink. High sugar content and low alcohol liquids will sink to the bottom, while low sugar high-proof liquor will float to the top. Once you know this, the only other thing you need to know to become an expert of layering drinks and cocktails is the technique.

You want to pour your high density/thicker liquid into your glass, then place a bar spoon right at the top of your high density liquid. You can then gently pour your low density liquid onto the spoon, slowly moving the spoon up as the liquid raises. And that is all it takes to create beautiful layered drinks!


TASTING SPIRITS

There is a lot to gain from tasting spirits, it's the only way to truly understand the spirit. What is it, it can do? And what makes it unique? Different variations of spirits offer different experiences, different undertones and the label doesn't always reveal a spirits identity. So here is a guide on how to taste your spirits!

- Pour a small amount of your spirit into a glass (preferably a tasting glass, also known as a tulip glass)
- You can add a little water if you would like, as it lowers the alcohol strength and can also help bring out certain elements of the spirit, like gin's botanicals!
- Swirl the glass, to oxidize the spirit and bring out flavor. Make sure to smell the spirit, before tasting it, and take notice of everything you can smell in the spirit.
- When it comes to tasting, you should sip a small amount of the spirit and give it time to settle in your mouth.
There is no reason to overcomplicate it, try to get the most out of your experience as having a good experience and having a good time is the most important part!


ICE - LOTS OF ICE!

It can't be said enough, you should add LOTS of ice to any iced drink! Don't worry about watering it down, more ice actually means it will take longer for your drink to water down, as ice cubes isolate themselves and take longer to melt. When you add your ice cubes to your glass, you should stir it for a little while as it cools down your glass. You can then pour out the water that collects at the bottom of the glass and then add your drink ingredients.
